Many students choose for taking a gap year instead of applying to universities after graduating. Youngsters generally possess a huge urge to explore, which is one of the reasons why high school students opt to work or travel before enrolling in a new academic affair. This essay will examine both advantages and disadvantages of this phenomenon.

A year being free from studying provides students opportunities to travel, encourages them to get out of their comfort zone. Meanwhile, students are able to experience distant regions, different cultures, which help to broaden their knowledge about cultural diversity. Furthermore, a number of students have been choosing to seek for a part-time job in their gap year. Working not only teaches them about the value of money but also helps to hone their skills in particular sectors which are relevant to their desired profession. For example, a job to sell products in a technological store could facilitate a boy to study Information Technologies major in the future.

On the other hand, a gap year poses potential risks. The biggest problem every gap year takers might encounter is allowance. Unless students have a good financial plan, they will suffer from the shortage of money during the gap year journey. Additionally, the students would face the adaptability difficulties once the gap year ends. Quite a long time has been apart from studying, they might find difficult to tackle their assignments at school.

In conclusion, besides the advantages that taking a gap year provides, it does have some detrimental impacts. Students must severely consider before taking risks.
